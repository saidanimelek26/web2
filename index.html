Solutions for Samsung Android Open Accessory Protocol Issues

Samsung AOA Transfer Error Solutions

Solution 1: Enable OTG Connection

On your Samsung device, go to:

Settings → Connections → USB utilities → Enable OTG connection

This is required for AOA to work on many Samsung devices.

Solution 2: USB Configuration Change

On your Samsung device, enable Developer Options then:

Settings → Developer options → Default USB configuration → Select "File Transfer"

Then reconnect your device.

Solution 3: Smart Switch Interference

Samsung Smart Switch may be blocking AOA access:

1. Disable Smart Switch if prompted on your device

2. Or uninstall Smart Switch on your computer

Reconnect your device after making these changes.

Solution 4: Driver Fix (Windows)

On Windows, you might need to change the driver:

1. Download Zadig from zadig.akeo.ie

2. Select your Samsung device

3. Install WinUSB driver

Then reconnect your device.

Important Note About Samsung Devices

Many Samsung devices have restrictions that prevent WebUSB from working properly with AOA protocol, even when the device supports AOA.

The solutions above work for many users, but some Samsung devices may require additional steps or may not work with WebUSB at all.

Alternative Approaches

If the solutions don't work, consider these alternatives:

  • Use a different Android device for testing
  • Try the native Linux ADK tool instead of WebUSB
  • Use a USB OTG cable instead of direct connection
  • Try a different USB port on your computer
